SHADeS++: Self-Consistent Specular Mask Prediction with Temporal Alignment

An improved formulation of specular-aware monocular depth estimation for endoscopy, replacing rigid binary masks with soft, temporally consistent specular weighting.

Minseok Kwak · UCL · Supervisor: Francisco Vasconcelos

SHADeS++ Code ↗ Original SHADeS Paper ↗
Abstract

Specular reflections significantly degrade the performance of monocular depth estimation models, particularly in endoscopic and other specular-heavy environments where photometric assumptions are frequently violated. Recent approaches such as SHADeS attempt to explicitly model specular components using auxiliary reprojection constraints, but remain limited by overly rigid masking strategies and unstable training dynamics. We present SHADeS++, an improved formulation of specular-aware depth estimation that introduces (i) a soft, temporally consistent specular masking mechanism and (ii) a self-consistent reprojection objective that jointly refines diffuse and specular components. Our method modifies the original SHADeS loss to replace hard binary masks with a temperature-controlled soft weighting scheme, enabling smoother gradients and improved generalization. Experiments on the C3VD dataset show that SHADeS++ achieves lower validation loss and visually cleaner depth reconstructions under heavy specular interference, while remaining compatible with the original SHADeS architecture.

01 Introduction

Monocular depth estimation has advanced rapidly with self-supervised frameworks based on photometric reprojection. These methods, however, fundamentally rely on a brightness constancy assumption that breaks down under specular reflection. In endoscopic imaging, specular highlights are not only frequent but structured, producing systematic depth errors rather than random noise. SHADeS addresses this by decomposing the input into diffuse and specular components and applying reprojection constraints selectively. While effective, the original formulation has two key limitations:

  1. Hard specular masking introduces discontinuous gradients and unstable optimization.
  2. Frame-wise independent masking ignores temporal consistency, causing flickering artifacts and inconsistent depth predictions.

These limitations motivate a refined formulation that preserves the core intuition of SHADeS while improving optimization behavior and temporal robustness.

02 Method

2.1 Overview

SHADeS++ retains the original SHADeS network architecture and focuses exclusively on reformulating the loss functions and intermediate representations. The core idea is to replace hard specular decisions with soft, temperature-controlled masks, and to enforce self-consistency across time in the specular-aware reprojection process.

2.2 Specular Masking

In the original SHADeS formulation, the specular mask is a binary indicator that excludes specular regions from the photometric loss. Let \( M_{\mathrm{hard}} \in \{0,1\}^{H \times W} \) denote this mask. We instead define a soft specular mask:

$$ M_{\mathrm{soft}} = \sigma\!\left( \frac{S - \mu}{\tau} \right) $$

where:

  • \( S \) denotes the predicted specular intensity,
  • \( \mu \) is a learned or fixed threshold,
  • \( \tau \) is a temperature parameter controlling mask sharpness,
  • \( \sigma \) is the sigmoid function.

This formulation ensures differentiability across all pixels, a gradual transition between diffuse and specular regions, and stable gradients during early training. Empirically, we find that \( \tau = 0.10 \) gives the best trade-off between selectivity and smoothness.

2.3 Self-Consistent Specular Reprojection

SHADeS introduces an auxiliary reprojection loss on the estimated specular component, but applies it independently per frame. In SHADeS++, we enforce self-consistency by requiring that the specular-aware reprojection remains stable across adjacent frames:

$$ \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{SC}} = \left\lVert M_{\mathrm{soft}}^{t} \odot \left( I^{t} - \hat{I}_{\mathrm{diff}}^{t \leftarrow t+1} \right) - M_{\mathrm{soft}}^{t+1} \odot \left( I^{t+1} - \hat{I}_{\mathrm{diff}}^{t+1 \leftarrow t} \right) \right\rVert_{1} $$

This structure encourages temporal coherence in specular estimation, fewer artifacts in depth maps, and more stable training dynamics.

2.4 Loss Function

The final loss function is:

$$ \mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{photo}}^{\mathrm{soft}} + \lambda_{\mathrm{SC}} \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{SC}} + \lambda_{\mathrm{smooth}} \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{smooth}} $$

where the photometric loss is weighted by \( M_{\mathrm{soft}} \) rather than masked by a binary indicator.

03 Experiments

3.1 Dataset and Setup

We evaluate on the C3VD and Hyper-Kvasir datasets, which contain severe and frequent specular reflections. Training and validation splits follow the original SHADeS protocol. All experiments use identical architectures and hyperparameters, except for the proposed loss modifications.
