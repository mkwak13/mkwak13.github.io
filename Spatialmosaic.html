SpatialMosaic: A Multi-view VLM Dataset for Partial Visibility

A multimodal 3D vision-language pipeline that learns spatial relations across multiple views, targeting scenes with object occlusion and partial visibility without explicit 3D reconstruction.

Minseok Kwak (second author) · VGI Lab, Seoul National University

arXiv Paper ↗ Project Page ↗
Abstract

SpatialMosaic is a multimodal 3D VLM pipeline that learns spatial relations under multi-view scenarios, focusing on frames with object occlusion and partial visibility where a single view is insufficient. Rather than relying on explicit 3D reconstruction, the method integrates the VGGT foundation model into its architecture to recover spatial structure implicitly, and performs among state-of-the-art models on VSI-Bench. The work is accompanied by a large multi-view dataset and benchmark designed around occlusion and edge-case visibility.

Full paper and code will be released after the review process.

01 Summary

Standard vision-language models struggle when objects are only partially visible or occluded across views, because the spatial relationships they need are spread across frames rather than present in any single image. SpatialMosaic addresses this by learning cross-view spatial locality directly, pairing a foundation-model backbone with a dataset and benchmark built specifically around partial-visibility cases.
