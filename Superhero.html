Overview

Created at the request of the National Autistic Society in cooperation with MotionInputGames, and developed under official partnership and strict supervision, SuperHero SportsDay is a 3D Unity game intended as a therapeutic tool for trained professionals. It targets the improvement of motor skills in middle-childhood students on the autism spectrum through motion-capture-driven, game-based activities.

02 Role & Build

As lead developer on a team of engineers, I coordinated the build and integrated real-time motion capture into Unity, mapping player movement onto in-game characters via rigidbody physics. The project ran in a formally contracted, client-centered setting with the NAS, where requirements, accessibility, and user experience for the target age group drove design decisions, and GPU optimization kept the experience responsive on target hardware.
