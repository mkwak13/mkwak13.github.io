Superhero Sportsday: Winter Edition

An inclusive winter sports game for neurodivergent children, played entirely with full-body motion. Eight winter activities, no controllers, all safe and engaging.

Minseok Kwak · Team Lead · UCL, with the National Autistic Society & MotionInputGames

Watch Demo ↗ Project Site ↗ GitHub ↗
Overview

Winter brings ice skating, sledding, and skiing, but for many neurodivergent children those activities can feel overwhelming. Superhero Sportsday: Winter Edition lets them experience the magic of winter in a safe, playful environment. Powered by MotionInput, children play with their whole body, with no controllers at all, across eight winter activities that keep them active while, most importantly, having fun. It was built in official partnership with the National Autistic Society and MotionInputGames.

02 Role & build

I led the team that designed and built Superhero Sportsday: Winter Edition, a 3D Unity game that turns full-body movement into play. The work ran in a client-centered partnership setting, where the experience of the children, accessibility, and a safe, welcoming feel guided every decision, from the activities we chose to how forgiving each interaction is.
